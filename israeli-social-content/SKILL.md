# Israeli Social Content

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Israeli Social Media Landscape

**Platform Usage in Israel (2024-2025):**
| Platform | Israeli Users (approx) | Primary Audience | Content Style |
|----------|----------------------|------------------|---------------|
| Facebook | ~6.5M | Ages 25-55, broadest reach | Groups, discussions, long-form, community |
| Instagram | ~4M | Ages 18-40, urban, lifestyle | Visual, Stories, Reels, aspirational |
| TikTok | ~3M | Ages 16-30, entertainment-first | Short video, trends, humor, raw |
| LinkedIn | ~3.5M | Professionals, tech, B2B | Thought leadership, career, industry |
| Twitter/X | ~1.5M | Tech, politics, journalists | Breaking news, opinions, threads |
| Threads | Growing | Early adopters, tech-savvy | Conversations, text-first |

**Key Israeli Social Media Behaviors:**
- Facebook groups are the dominant discovery and discussion channel (unlike most Western markets)
- Israelis engage heavily in comments; comment sections are often more valuable than posts
- WhatsApp is the primary messaging platform and a major content sharing channel
- Hebrew content consistently outperforms English content for Israeli audiences
- Israelis are highly opinionated and engage well with controversial/debate-style content

### Step 2: Platform-Specific Content Strategy

**Facebook (Still #1 in Israel):**
- **Groups are king:** Identify relevant Israeli Facebook groups (some have 100K+ members)
  - "נדל"ן למשקיע הקטן" (Real estate, 400K+ members)
  - "מתכונים ובישול" (Recipes, 500K+ members)
  - Tech groups: "הייטקיסטים" (Hightechists), "סטארטאפיסטים"
- **Post types that work:** Personal stories (sipurim ishiyim), hot takes (da'ot hadot), advice requests
- **Engagement bait done right:** Ask genuine questions, "mi po she..." (who here...), polls
- **Optimal length:** 3-5 paragraphs; not too long, not too short
- **Emojis:** Used moderately; flags and relevant icons work well

**Instagram Israel:**
- **Reels dominate** reach; static posts for community building
- **Hebrew captions** with line breaks for readability
- **Hashtag strategy:** Mix Hebrew and English hashtags
- **Stories:** Behind-the-scenes, polls, questions; Israelis love interactive Stories
- **Collab posts:** Israeli influencer collaborations are very effective

**TikTok Israel:**
- **Hebrew audio** performs best; dubbing or subtitling is essential
- **Trending sounds:** Monitor Israeli TikTok trends separately from global
- **Humor style:** Self-deprecating, army humor, cultural stereotypes (done tastefully)
- **Local trends:** Israeli holidays, army references, food culture, regional stereotypes
- **Duration:** 15-60 seconds performs best for Israeli audience

**LinkedIn Israel:**
- **Tech ecosystem content** performs exceptionally well (Israel = "Startup Nation")
- **Hebrew vs English:** English acceptable for tech/international; Hebrew for local business
- **Post types:** Career stories, industry insights, hiring announcements
- **Engagement:** Israeli LinkedIn is more casual than global LinkedIn

### Step 3: Write Hebrew Social Copy

**Register and Tone:**
| Platform | Register | Tone | Example |
|----------|----------|------|---------|
| Facebook | Informal/Conversational | Warm, opinionated | "שמעו, אני חייב לספר לכם משהו..." |
| Instagram | Casual/Inspirational | Visual, aspirational | "הרגע הזה שאתם מבינים ש... ✨" |
| TikTok | Very Informal/Slang | Funny, relatable | "כשאמא שלי שואלת למה אני..." |
| LinkedIn | Business Casual | Professional, insightful | "3 דברים שלמדתי אחרי 10 שנים בהייטק" |

**Hebrew Social Copy Rules:**
- Write in spoken Hebrew (ivrit meduberet), not literary Hebrew
- Use contractions naturally: "שלא" not "שלא", "בטח" not "בוודאי" (formal)
- OK to sprinkle English tech/marketing terms that Israelis use daily
- Paragraph breaks are crucial for mobile readability
- First line is your hook; must stop the scroll

**Hook Formulas for Israeli Audience:**
```
1. Provocative statement: "אני הולך להגיד משהו שאנשים לא אוהבים לשמוע..."
   (I'm going to say something people don't like to hear...)

2. Story opener: "לפני שנה קרה לי משהו ששינה לי את הראש"
   (A year ago something happened that changed my perspective)

3. Listicle: "5 דברים שאף אחד לא מספר לכם על [נושא]"
   (5 things nobody tells you about [topic])

4. Question: "מי פה עבר את זה? [situation]"
   (Who here has been through this? [situation])

5. Contrarian: "כולם עושים [X]. אני עושה את ההפך. הנה למה:"
   (Everyone does [X]. I do the opposite. Here's why:)
```

### Step 4: Israeli Hashtag Strategy

**Hebrew Hashtag Best Practices:**
- No spaces in Hebrew hashtags (obviously): #שיווקדיגיטלי not #שיווק_דיגיטלי
- Underscore breaks the hashtag in Hebrew; use combined words
- Mix popular and niche hashtags

### Step 5: Content Calendar for Israeli Market

**Israeli Content Calendar Pillars:**
- **Jewish Holidays:** Rosh Hashana, Yom Kippur, Sukkot, Hanukkah, Purim, Pesach, Shavuot
- **National Days:** Independence Day (Yom Ha'atzmaut), Memorial Day (Yom HaZikaron), Holocaust Remembrance (Yom HaShoah)
- **Secular Events:** Back to school (September), summer vacation (July-August), Black Friday (growing trend)
- **Weekly Rhythm:** Sunday = new week energy; Thursday = weekend plans; Friday = Shabbat content

**Posting Schedule for Israel:**
| Day | Best Times (Israel Time, UTC+2/+3) | Notes |
|-----|-----------------------------------|-------|
| Sunday | 8-9 AM, 12-1 PM, 7-9 PM | Start of work week; high engagement |
| Monday-Wednesday | 12-1 PM, 7-9 PM | Steady engagement |
| Thursday | 12-1 PM, 4-6 PM | Pre-weekend, lifestyle content |
| Friday | 8-10 AM only | Engagement drops sharply by noon |
| Saturday | 8-10 PM (after Shabbat) | Post-Shabbat spike in engagement |

### Step 6: Content Formats That Work in Israel

**High-Performing Formats:**
1. **Personal story + lesson** (Facebook, LinkedIn) - Israelis love authentic stories
2. **Before/after** (Instagram) - Visual transformation content
3. **Hot take / opinion** (Twitter/X, Facebook) - Debate drives engagement
4. **Tutorial / how-to** (TikTok, Instagram Reels) - Educational content in Hebrew
5. **Behind the scenes** (Instagram Stories, TikTok) - Raw, unpolished content
6. **User-generated content** (All platforms) - Testimonials, reviews from Israeli customers

**Content Topics That Resonate in Israel:**
- Military/reserve service experiences
- Israeli food culture and recipes
- Startup ecosystem and tech news
- Housing market and cost of living
- Travel within Israel and abroad
- Education and career development

## Troubleshooting

- **Issue**: Low reach on Hebrew posts
  **Solution**: Facebook algorithm may favor shorter posts. Test different post lengths. Ensure you're posting at Israeli peak hours (not US times). Check if your page has Hebrew set as primary language.

- **Issue**: Low engagement in Israeli Facebook groups
  **Solution**: Groups have cultural norms. Lurk first, understand the culture, then contribute value before promoting. Direct sales posts are usually deleted. Ask questions, share experiences, build reputation.

- **Issue**: Hashtags not working on Instagram
  **Solution**: Hebrew hashtags can break if there are spaces or underscores. Verify formatting. Mix 5-10 relevant hashtags (not 30). Place in first comment if preferred by your audience.

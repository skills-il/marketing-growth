# Israeli Content Marketing

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Israeli Content Landscape

**Israeli Media Outlets for Content Distribution:**
| Outlet | Type | Audience | Content Fit |
|--------|------|----------|-------------|
| Geektime | Tech blog/media | Developers, startups, tech workers | Product launches, tech trends, startup stories |
| Calcalist Tech (כלכליסט טק) | Business + tech | Business professionals, investors | Fundraising news, market analysis, exec interviews |
| The Marker (דה מרקר) | Business/economics | Business leaders, investors, finance | Industry reports, economic analysis, enterprise |
| Globes (גלובס) | Business newspaper | Business community | Company profiles, deals, market trends |
| Ynet Tech | General tech | Broad Israeli audience | Consumer tech, apps, digital lifestyle |
| Walla Tech | General tech | Broad audience | Consumer tech, reviews, how-to guides |
| CTech (Calcalist English) | English tech news | International audience, investors | Israeli startups, global tech, English content |
| Startup Nation Central | Ecosystem platform | International investors, partners | Israeli innovation, ecosystem data |
| Mako Tech | Consumer tech | Mainstream Israeli audience | Reviews, comparisons, buying guides |
| No Camels | English blog | International tech community | Israeli innovation stories in English |

**Israeli Content Consumption Patterns:**
- Mobile-first: Over 70% of Israeli content is consumed on mobile devices
- Hebrew content dominates for B2C; B2B tech content splits between Hebrew and English
- Facebook groups are a major content discovery and sharing channel
- WhatsApp sharing is significant for viral content reach
- Israeli readers prefer direct, concise writing; overly formal content underperforms
- Video content (YouTube, TikTok) is growing rapidly in Israel

### Step 2: Hebrew SEO Content Strategy

**Hebrew Keyword Research:**
1. Use Google Keyword Planner with location set to Israel
2. Check Google Trends Israel (trends.google.co.il) for seasonal patterns
3. Research Hebrew morphological variants (root-based language)
4. Identify bilingual search patterns (Israelis search in both Hebrew and English)

**Hebrew Content Structure for SEO:**
```
Title (H1): Primary keyword near beginning, 50-60 Hebrew characters
Meta description: 120-150 characters with keyword and CTA
URL slug: Transliterated Hebrew or English equivalent
  Good: /bituach-briut-hashva'a or /health-insurance-comparison
  Avoid: /%D7%91%D7%99%D7%98%D7%95%D7%97 (encoded Hebrew in URL)

Content structure:
- H2 headings every 200-300 words (Hebrew text appears denser)
- Short paragraphs (2-3 sentences)
- Bulleted lists for scannability
- Internal links with Hebrew anchor text
- Images with Hebrew alt text
```

**Hebrew On-Page SEO Checklist:**
- [ ] Primary keyword in H1 (naturally, not forced)
- [ ] Secondary keywords in H2 subheadings
- [ ] Meta description with keyword, 120-150 Hebrew characters
- [ ] URL slug: transliterated or English
- [ ] Image alt text in Hebrew
- [ ] Internal links to related Hebrew content
- [ ] External links to authoritative Hebrew/Israeli sources
- [ ] Schema markup (FAQ, HowTo, Article)
- [ ] Mobile-optimized layout (Hebrew text can appear cramped on mobile)
- [ ] Reading time estimate displayed (Israelis appreciate knowing time commitment)

### Step 4: Israeli Tech PR and Media Outreach

**Pitch Template for Israeli Tech Journalists:**
```
Subject: [Company Name] - [One-line headline of news]

Hi [Journalist name],

[1 sentence: What is the news / announcement]
[1 sentence: Why it matters for Israeli market / global tech]
[1 sentence: Key data point or differentiator]

Quick facts:
- Founded: [Year], [City, Israel]
- Team: [Number] employees
- Funding: [Amount raised, investors]
- Customers: [Notable Israeli/global customers]
- Traction: [Key metric]

[Link to press kit / more info]

Happy to schedule a call or provide additional details.
[Your name]
```

| Event | Timing | Content Opportunity |

- Israeli journalists prefer direct, brief pitches (no flowery language)

- Embargo culture exists but is less formal than in the US
- Hebrew pitches for Hebrew-language outlets; English for CTech, No Camels
- Israeli journalists are highly connected; news travels fast in the small ecosystem

## Troubleshooting

- **Issue**: Hebrew blog posts not ranking in Google Israel
  **Solution**: Check Google Search Console for Israel-specific data. Ensure content targets Hebrew keywords (not translated English keywords). Build Hebrew internal links. Submit sitemap with hreflang tags for Hebrew content.

- **Issue**: Low engagement on Israeli social media shares
  **Solution**: Israeli audiences engage more with opinionated, personal content than corporate messaging. Rewrite social shares in first person, add a hot take or question. Post during Israeli peak hours (Sunday-Thursday, 9-10 AM or 7-8 PM).

- **Issue**: Israeli tech media not responding to pitches
  **Solution**: Pitches may be too long or too generic. Israeli journalists prefer brief, direct pitches with clear news value. Include concrete data. Follow up via WhatsApp (common in Israeli tech journalism). Make sure the news is genuinely newsworthy for the Israeli market.

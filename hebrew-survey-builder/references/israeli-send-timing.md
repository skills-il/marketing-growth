# When to Send Surveys in Israel

Israeli response patterns are shaped by the Jewish work week, religious observances, and cultural rhythms. Sending at the wrong time tanks your response rate even if the questions are perfect. This reference is about *when* to push a survey, not *what* to put in it (see `hebrew-survey-templates.md` for content).

## Day of Week

| Day | Verdict | Why |
|-----|---------|-----|
| Sunday | Best | Start of the Israeli work week. Inboxes are fresh, not yet cluttered. |
| Monday–Wednesday | Good | Steady engagement. Tuesday and Wednesday are typically the best days for B2B. |
| Thursday | Acceptable, leaning late | People are wrapping up the week; less attention for non-urgent items. |
| Friday (morning) | Risky | Short workday for most Israelis. Many offices close by noon or run half-day. |
| Friday (afternoon) | Avoid | Shabbat prep begins. Observant recipients are offline. |
| Shabbat (Fri evening through Sat evening) | Do not send | Observant recipients will not see it at all; by Sunday it is buried. |
| Saturday evening (מוצאי שבת) | Acceptable for younger/less-observant audiences | Works for tech/millennial audiences; avoid for broader market. |

## Time of Day

For Sunday through Thursday:

- **Morning (9:00–11:00)** — Strong window. Morning coffee, before the day gets hectic.
- **Early afternoon (13:00–14:00)** — Second best. Post-lunch lull, good for quick surveys.
- **Late afternoon (after 17:00)** — Weaker. People are wrapping up / commuting.
- **Before 08:30 or after 20:00** — Avoid unless your audience is specifically known to be online then (night-shift workers, global remote teams).

## Jewish Holidays (Chagim)

Avoid sending surveys during these weeks — response rates collapse because people are with family or traveling. Exact dates shift each year per the Hebrew calendar; always check before scheduling a campaign.

- **Rosh Hashanah** (2 days, September or October) — the 10 days leading into Yom Kippur are also soft
- **Yom Kippur** — no one will respond on the day itself
- **Sukkot** (week-long, ending with Simchat Torah)
- **Passover / Pesach** (week-long, April)
- **Shavuot** (1 day, late May or June, surrounding days are soft)
- **Yom HaZikaron → Yom HaAtzmaut** (Memorial Day into Independence Day, back-to-back) — emotionally heavy, do not send

The two week-long holidays (Sukkot and Pesach) are especially bad. Offices run on skeleton crews or close entirely.

## Post-Event and Transactional Surveys

- **Post-event feedback** — Send within 24 hours of the event ending, while details are fresh. If the event ended on a Thursday evening, wait until Sunday morning rather than sending Friday.
- **CSAT after support ticket** — Send immediately after ticket resolution while the interaction is still in mind. Do NOT batch-send at fixed daily times.
- **NPS relationship survey** — Pick one good slot (e.g., Tuesday or Wednesday, mid-morning) and hold it constant across quarters so results are comparable.
- **Onboarding CSAT** — A 7-day post-signup cadence is common, but shift it off Friday/Saturday so the survey lands mid-week.

## Frequency / Cadence

- **NPS relationship surveys** — quarterly is standard; monthly feels spammy to Israeli B2B audiences
- **CSAT per ticket** — one per ticket, never per support agent
- **Product discovery interviews** — never back-to-back with the same user; leave at least 2 weeks of breathing room

## Audience Caveat

These rules apply to Hebrew surveys sent to Israeli audiences. If your audience is specifically global-English-speaking Israelis (e.g., expats, tech workers in multinational companies, offshore engineers), day-of-week patterns shift slightly toward Western Monday–Friday norms. The chag warnings still apply — those hit regardless of language.

## Quick Decision Tree

1. Is it a chag week? → Delay until after the chag.
2. Is it Friday after lunch or Shabbat? → Delay until Sunday morning.
3. Is it a transactional survey (post-ticket, post-event)? → Send now, as close to the interaction as possible.
4. Is it a relationship survey (NPS, quarterly CSAT)? → Tuesday or Wednesday, 9:30–11:00.
5. Unsure? → Sunday 10:00 is the safest default.

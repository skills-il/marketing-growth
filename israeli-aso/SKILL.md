# Israeli ASO (App Store Optimization)

## Instructions

### Step 1: Understand the Israeli Mobile Market

**Israeli Market Characteristics:**
- Israel has ~10M population with ~8.5M smartphone users (~85% penetration)
- iOS vs Android split: approximately 30% iOS, 70% Android (Android-dominant)
- Hebrew is the primary language; apps without Hebrew localization lose significant market share
- Israeli users are early adopters and tech-savvy; high app download rates per capita
- Key app categories in Israel: fintech, transportation (Moovit, Gett), food delivery (Wolt, 10bis), super-apps

**Localization Priority:**
| Element | Priority | Impact on Downloads |
|---------|----------|-------------------|
| App name/title | Critical | 30-40% impact |
| Keywords (iOS) | Critical | 25-35% impact |
| Short description (Google Play) | High | 15-25% impact |
| Full description | High | 10-20% impact |
| Screenshots | Critical | 20-30% impact |
| Video preview | Medium | 10-15% impact |
| What's New | Low | 5% impact |

### Step 2: Hebrew App Title and Subtitle

**App Title Rules:**
- **Apple App Store:** 30 characters max for title
- **Google Play:** 30 characters max for title
- Include primary Hebrew keyword in title when possible
- Use the Hebrew brand name if established; otherwise, keep English brand + Hebrew descriptor

**Title Patterns for Israeli Market:**
```
Pattern 1: [Brand] - [Hebrew Descriptor]
Example: Colu - ארנק דיגיטלי (Colu - Digital Wallet)

Pattern 2: [Hebrew Name] [Category]
Example: תן ביס - משלוחי אוכל (10bis - Food Delivery)

Pattern 3: [English Brand] | [Hebrew Keyword]
Example: Monday | ניהול פרויקטים (Monday | Project Management)
```

**Subtitle (iOS) / Short Description (Google Play):**
- 30 characters (iOS subtitle), 80 characters (Google Play short description)
- Include secondary Hebrew keywords
- Focus on primary value proposition in Hebrew
- Examples:
  - "הזמנת אוכל ומשלוחים מהירים" (Fast food ordering and delivery)
  - "ניהול כספים חכם ופשוט" (Smart and simple money management)

### Step 3: Hebrew Keyword Research for App Stores

**Apple App Store Keywords (100 characters):**
- Hebrew characters count same as English in character limit
- Separate keywords with commas (no spaces after comma to save characters)
- No need to repeat words already in title
- Include common Hebrew misspellings and alternative forms

**Keyword Research Process:**
1. **Brainstorm Hebrew terms:** Think of how Israelis would search
   - Formal term: "ניווט" (navigation)
   - Colloquial: "וייז" (Waze), "ניווט GPS"
   - Action-oriented: "מצא דרך", "הגעה ל"

2. **Morphological variants:** Hebrew roots generate multiple forms
   - חשבון (account) -> חשבונות (accounts), חשבונאות (accounting)
   - תשלום (payment) -> תשלומים (payments), לשלם (to pay)

3. **Bilingual keywords:** Israelis search in both languages
   - Include English terms Israelis commonly use: "delivery", "fitness", "shopping"
   - Include Hebrew transliterations: "דליברי", "פיטנס", "שופינג"

4. **Competitor analysis:** Check top Israeli apps in your category
   - What Hebrew keywords do competitors use in their titles?
   - What terms appear in Israeli user reviews?

**Google Play Description Keywords:**
- Google Play indexes the full description (unlike iOS)
- Naturally integrate Hebrew keywords throughout
- Repeat primary keywords 3-5 times without keyword stuffing
- Use keyword density of 2-3% for primary terms

### Step 4: Hebrew Screenshot and Visual Optimization

**RTL Screenshot Design:**
- Text overlays must be RTL-aligned (right-to-left)
- Hebrew text should use standard Israeli fonts (not decorative Hebrew fonts)
- Reading flow: start from the RIGHT screenshot
- Apple requires screenshots to flow in reading order; RTL means right to left

**Screenshot Best Practices for Israeli Users:**
| Screenshot # | Purpose | Content Suggestion |
|-------------|---------|-------------------|
| 1 (rightmost) | Hero/Value Proposition | Bold Hebrew headline with main benefit |
| 2 | Core Feature | Most important feature demo |
| 3 | Social Proof | "500,000+ משתמשים בישראל" (500K+ users in Israel) |
| 4 | Secondary Feature | Unique differentiator |
| 5 | Call to Action | "הורידו עכשיו בחינם" (Download now for free) |

**Text Overlay Guidelines:**
- Keep headlines to 3-5 Hebrew words
- Use large, bold fonts (minimum 60pt equivalent)
- Ensure high contrast (Hebrew text on complex backgrounds is hard to read)
- Recommended fonts: Heebo, Rubik, Assistant (Google Fonts with Hebrew support)
- Include NIS prices where relevant: ₪0 / חינם (free)

**Color Considerations:**
- Israeli apps tend to use blue (trust, national color), white, and modern gradients
- Avoid heavy red (aggressive connotation in Israeli market)
- Bright, clean designs perform better than dark themes in screenshots

### Step 5: Hebrew App Description Writing

**Full Description Structure (Google Play):**
```
[Opening hook - 1-2 sentences with primary keyword]

[Value proposition paragraph - what makes the app unique]

[Feature list with Hebrew bullets:]
- ✅ [Feature 1 with keyword]
- ✅ [Feature 2 with keyword]
- ✅ [Feature 3 with keyword]

[Social proof paragraph - Israeli user numbers, awards, press mentions]

[How it works - simple 3-step explanation]

[Closing CTA - download encouragement]
```

**Hebrew Description Copy Tips:**
- Write in informal, friendly Hebrew (guf sheni - "you" form)
- Use emojis strategically (1-2 per section, not excessive)
- Include Israeli-specific social proof: "מומלצת על ידי גלובס/כלכליסט" (Recommended by Globes/Calcalist)
- Mention Israeli-specific features: shekel payments, Hebrew interface, Israeli customer support
- Address Israeli concerns: privacy, security, no hidden fees ("ללא עלויות נסתרות")

### Step 6: Ratings and Reviews Strategy for Israel

**Israeli Review Patterns:**
- Israeli users are more likely to leave negative reviews than positive ones
- Hebrew reviews strongly influence Israeli users' download decisions
- Users expect fast response to reviews in Hebrew
- Rating prompts work best after a positive in-app experience (not on first open)

**Review Response Template (Hebrew):**
```
Positive review response:
"תודה רבה על הביקורת! שמחים שנהנית/נהנה מהאפליקציה 🙏"

Negative review response:
"תודה על המשוב. אנחנו מצטערים על חוויה שלא עמדה בציפיות.
צוות התמיכה שלנו ישמח לעזור - פנו אלינו ב-[email/phone].
בברכה, צוות [App Name]"
```

**Rating Optimization:**
- Request ratings after positive moments (successful order, achievement, etc.)
- Never request ratings during onboarding or error states
- Israeli users appreciate directness: "אהבת? דרגו אותנו!" (Liked it? Rate us!)
- Respond to all Hebrew reviews within 24-48 hours

## Troubleshooting

- **Issue**: Hebrew text appears backwards or broken in screenshots
  **Solution**: Ensure design tool supports RTL text. Use Figma or Sketch with Hebrew font. Test text rendering. Avoid mixing Hebrew and English in the same text line where possible.

- **Issue**: Hebrew keywords not being indexed on iOS
  **Solution**: Check total keyword field length (100 chars max). Remove spaces after commas. Ensure keywords are not already in the title (Apple ignores duplicates). Use singular forms; Apple matches plural variants.

- **Issue**: Low conversion rate despite good rankings
  **Solution**: Check screenshot quality on actual phone screens. Ensure Hebrew descriptions are in informal register. Add Israeli social proof. Verify that the first 1-3 screenshots clearly communicate the value proposition in Hebrew.

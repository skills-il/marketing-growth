# Israeli Mobile App Market

## Market Overview
- ~8.7M smartphone users (~91% penetration)
- Android: ~67%, iOS: ~33% (StatCounter, 2026)
- Average apps per device: 40+
- App revenue per user: Higher than MENA average

## Apple App Store (Israel)
- Keyword field: 100 characters, per-localization (the Hebrew localization has its own separate field)
- Title: 30 characters
- Subtitle: 30 characters
- Indexed fields: app name, subtitle, keyword field (the description is NOT indexed)
- Localization: he (Hebrew). Hebrew is indexed only in the Israel storefront, so there is no cross-localization keyword carryover to lean on (unlike, e.g., Arabic which spans many storefronts). The Hebrew keyword field must stand entirely on its own.
- Currency: ILS (₪)

## Google Play (Israel)
- Title: 30 characters (no promotional words like "best"/"#1"/"free"/"top", no calls to action, no emoji, no ALL-CAPS)
- Short description: 80 characters (a high-weight indexed field, pack the primary Hebrew keyword here)
- Full description: 4,000 characters
- Indexed fields: title, short description, full description (no separate keyword field)
- Localization: Google Play Console uses `iw-IL` for Hebrew. `iw` is the legacy ISO 639 alias; `he` / `he-IL` is the modern standard code, but the Console listing still shows and accepts `iw-IL`. Verify the exact code in the current Play Console UI.
- Keywords extracted from the description text

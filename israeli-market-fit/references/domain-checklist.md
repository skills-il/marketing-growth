# Domain Coverage Checklist - israeli-market-fit

Generated: 2026-06-02 via research on: cbs.gov.il (population release), Israel Democracy Institute Haredi Yearbook 2025, taxsummaries.pwc.com (VAT), sii.org.il (standards), nevo.co.il + gov.il (consumer and privacy law), Globes (payment apps), Wikipedia (Yad2), Kol-Zchut (average wage).

## Must cover (core)

- [ ] Israeli market segmentation - split the addressable market into real sub-markets, not "Israel = one market": secular/traditional Hebrew mainstream, Haredi/ultra-Orthodox (14.3% of the population, distinct media, channels, kashrut/modesty gates), Arab-Israeli (21.1%, Arabic-first, separate retail/social graph), new olim (Hebrew-light, diaspora habits), Russian-speaking, periphery/development towns vs Gush Dan center. - source: https://www.idi.org.il/haredi/2025/?chapter=62168 - why core: a product that fits Tel Aviv often dies in Bnei Brak or Nazareth.
- [ ] Market sizing method (TAM/SAM/SOM, bottom-up) - convert segment population times adoption times spend into a credible serviceable number; flag that Israel's whole market is about 10.178 million people, so global SaaS economics rarely translate. - source: https://www.cbs.gov.il/he/mediarelease/DocLib/2025/422/01_25_422b.pdf - why core: the verdict hinges on whether the SOM clears a revenue floor.
- [ ] Payment and purchasing culture - P2P/app payment is dominated by Bit (Bank Hapoalim) and paybox (Discount Bank); cash still matters in Haredi/Arab segments; credit-card installments (תשלומים) are an expectation, not a perk. - source: https://en.globes.co.il/en/article-payment-apps-bit-and-paybox-to-charge-fees-from-january-1001483104 - why core: a checkout that omits Bit or installments loses conversions.
- [ ] Distribution / channel reality - where Israelis actually transact: Yad2 (dominant horizontal classifieds), WhatsApp commerce, Facebook groups, local marketplaces vs thin domestic app-store volume. - source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yad2 - why core: "build it and they will come" fails; channel fit is part of market fit.
- [ ] Regulatory gates to bring a product to market - an official standard (תקן רשמי) enforced by SII/Ministry of Economy blocks import or sale of regulated goods until lab-tested. - source: https://www.sii.org.il/he/israelistandards/ - why core: a physical product can be commercially dead on arrival without an official-standard path.
- [ ] Hebrew labeling requirement - every consumer-product package must carry clear Hebrew labeling even when imported. - source: https://weksler-davidovich.co.il/consumer-protection-labeling/ - why core: non-negotiable cost and operational gate for any physical consumer good.
- [ ] Consumer Protection Law - bans deceptive marketing and mandates final-price display. - source: https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law00/70305.htm - why core: defines the legal envelope for marketing claims and pricing UI.
- [ ] Direct-marketing / consent regime - Privacy Protection Law Amendment 13 (in force 14 August 2025) plus the anti-spam law require active, prior consent for promotional email/SMS. - source: https://www.gov.il/he/pages/13_amendment - why core: the email/SMS go-to-market channel is legally gated upfront.
- [ ] Pricing localization - price in shekels, VAT-inclusive at 18%, benchmarked against the local average wage (13,566 NIS/month as of January 2026), installment-friendly. - source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/israel/corporate/other-taxes - why core: a price that ignores VAT and local wages reads as overpriced or illegal (non-final price).
- [ ] Seasonality - demand swings around the Jewish-holiday calendar (Rosh Hashana, Pesach, Sukkot), Ramadan for the Arab segment, summer/August, and reserve-duty (miluim) disruption. - source: https://www.cbs.gov.il/he/mediarelease/DocLib/2025/422/01_25_422b.pdf - why core: launch and ad-spend timing depend on the local calendar.
- [ ] Buyer psychology and trust signals - Israeli buyers are price-aggressive, recommendation-driven (WhatsApp word-of-mouth), skeptical of hype; Hebrew UI and local support raise trust. - source: https://www.nevo.co.il/law_html/law00/70305.htm - why core: positioning angles must match how Israelis decide.
- [ ] Competitor / substitute scan - map local incumbents and the "good enough free WhatsApp/Excel/Yad2" substitute before claiming a gap. - source: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yad2 - why core: most "no competitor in Israel" claims are wrong once local substitutes are listed.
- [ ] Buyer-avatar construction - segment-specific avatar cards (language, income band, channels, objections, trust triggers, payment method), not one generic persona. - source: https://www.idi.org.il/haredi/2025/?chapter=62168 - why core: this is the skill's core output, grounded in real segment data (Haredi median age 16, 57% under 20).
- [ ] Viability verdict framework (go / pivot / no-go) - an explicit decision rule combining SOM size, regulatory-gate severity, channel fit, and competitive density. - source: https://www.cbs.gov.il/he/mediarelease/DocLib/2025/422/01_25_422b.pdf - why core: this is the deliverable the user actually wants.
- [ ] Global-vs-Israel delta - contrast what works in the global/US market against Israeli realities (much smaller market, RTL/Hebrew, VAT-inclusive pricing, holiday calendar, regulatory gates) and name what must change. - source: https://taxsummaries.pwc.com/israel/corporate/other-taxes - why core: the skill exists to translate a global thesis into an Israel-specific verdict.

## Should cover (advanced / edge cases)

- [ ] RTL/Hebrew product and UX readiness - Hebrew plus Arabic localization, RTL layout, Hebrew SEO as a fit precondition.
- [ ] Diaspora / dual-market plays - olim and Russian/English-speaking sub-markets that bridge Israel and abroad.
- [ ] Kashrut / modesty / religious-fit gates - for food, fashion, media, leisure aimed at observant segments.
- [ ] Import duty / customs path - duties and purchase tax on top of VAT for imported goods affect landed cost.
- [ ] B2B / SaaS / government GTM - size by accounts not population; tiny domestic logo count; Israel often a design-partner/beachhead not a revenue market; tenders (מכרז), resellers, integrators; NIS vs USD contracting.
- [ ] Parallel / grey imports and the single-importer model - for physical brands the competitor is often a grey-market parallel importer or the entrenched official importer.
- [ ] Retail-chain gatekeepers - getting on a shelf often runs through one importer/distributor and chains such as Shufersal, Rami Levy, Super-Pharm.
- [ ] Personal-import (de minimis) substitute - buyers ordering directly from foreign sites under the personal-import tax exemption are a real competitor to local sellers.
- [ ] Payment-rail mechanics - cards via local gateways/acquirers process installments; foreign gateways may not support Israeli installments; Bit/paybox are P2P, not the business checkout.
- [ ] Mean-vs-median wage caveat - the average wage overstates affordability; benchmark to local substitute prices, not the mean.
- [ ] National-religious (dati-leumi) segment - mainstream-reachable but with kashrut/values filters, distinct from secular and Haredi.
- [ ] Reserve-duty / wartime demand shocks - miluim call-ups and security events distort B2C demand.
- [ ] Periphery logistics and delivery economics - last-mile cost and coverage gaps outside the center.
